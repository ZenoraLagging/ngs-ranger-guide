Weapon Overview

Assault Rifles

Mid-range mobile assaulter

Rifle is a middle-range specialist that relies on staying within optimal distance for maximum effectiveness. Allows access to Weak Bullet, which places a marker on enemies for +20% more damage.
Because of their fast animations, they are best at dealing with enemy aggro.

Optimal Distance Mechanic Explained Sample Rifle Rotation

Grenade Launchers

Slow, powerful demolitionist

Launchers are the heavy-duty damage dealers that relies on committing attacks for maximum DPS.
Relies on good PP management to sustain their main damage option, Fear Eraser
Because of their sluggish animations, they shine best during sandbag situations.

Photon Arts

Assault Rifles

Revolt Aim

New!
Main sandbag DPS

Fires lasers hitting up to 5 times at a fixed distance.
Difficult to use, but has one of the highest DPS on stationary targets.

Raging Shot

DPS Skill

Shoots a explosive round. Can be charged to pierce through enemies.
Uncharged version has better tracking on mobile enemies, but has worse PP efficiency.

Homing Dart

AoE Skill

Locks up to 10 targets, before firing off homing shots.

Blaze

Situational Repositioning Tool

Twirls around targets before firing a powerful shot. Can be held to go into a movable shooting stance.
Can be used to kite targets (and reinforcing Weak Bullet while soloing), but the lengthy animation and PP consumption holds it back greatly.

Grenade Launchers

Divine Impact

New!
Main AoE Skill

Fires a blast in front of you.
Charging this attack fires a powerful grenade instead

Fallen Impact

Situational

Fires a delayed mortar that lands after 2 seconds. Only one can exist at any time.

Fear Eraser

Main DPS Skill

Channels a powerful laser that continuously drains PP while held.
Adds a secondary attack when held for 3 seconds or longer.

Multi-Launch

Hitscan

Fires a barrage of rockets that has great tracking against enemies.
Has great PP efficiency, and can be dash-cancelled up until the 6th shot.

Gearing Up

Min-max

Rugged

HEY REMEMBER NEMESIS AND RAVEN SERIES? NOW IT'S BACK!
Currently the strongest series at this time of writing, Rugged has separate effects, whichever weapon is the base will take effect (when multi-weaponing):

Rifle offers Citadel Unit, which generates up to 3 40% damage resistant barrier every 30 seconds (kills lower the cooldown by 2s),
while Launcher offers Gyrating Unit, which grants 30% PP reduction for 20 seconds when consuming 300 PP (10s cooldown).

It's personal preference on which weapon you want inherit as base, but some considerations:
1. Kvaris region hurts and Rangers are very vulnerable due to animation locks so damage resistance is always useful, but
2. PP reduction allows for longer rotations without needing to refill PP, but Fear Eraser and Blaze Shot (don't use this shitty PA regardless) is not affected by PP Reduction.

Variance: 50% ~ 100%
Rifle: Citadel Unit (survive longer)
Launcher: Gyrating Unit (deal more DPS)
Fixa Fatale recommended

Strong

Rokz

The current best weapon of the 6 star series; notably, the Rifle grants 20% PP recovery when taking damage, while Launcher grants a 20% Attack PP Recovery when hitting something.
You only care about Launcher to use as the base for multi-weapons, as Attack PP Recovery affects PP recovered from Launcher charged shots, counters, and all grenades!!!
Provides the highest base attack of the 6 star series as well as highest potency in general making it the strongest 6 star weapon.

Variance: 75% ~ 100%
Rifle: Revolutionary Unit
Launcher: Staccato Unit (recommended)
Fixa Attack recommended

All-Arounder

Sechetyl

The mobbing weapon. This weapon provides 22% potency and a further 6% more against enemies that are not bosses. This is the best weapon for mobbing and is also unironically better than the Evoleclipse series due to its higher base attack.

Variance: 75% ~ 100%
Trample Unit
Fixa Attack recommended

Budget

Evoleclipse

Free 6 star weapon obtainable from the Kvaris Expedition Ticket Shop if you were playing prior to Kvaris-launch; unobtainable otherwise. Provides 22% potency as well as a 100% natural PP recovery upon a successful Sidestep dodge, but natural PP recovery doesn't benefit Ranger in most scenarios. Along with the newer skills for Kvaris, this potential is almost worthless as dodging with Rifle's WA is higher DPS than a regular step dodge...

Variance: 75% ~ 100%
Elusive Unit
Fixa Attack recommended

Class Combos

Recommended

Force

PP Battery
Usable Weapon(s)
Open Skill Tree

Offers great PP economy by boosting your natural PP regen through passives and actives
(150% passively, multiplies by 600% when activating PP Convert)
Note that you can't regen PP while using PAs.
Also allows rod, talis, and tech access to exploit element weaknesses for additional stuns.

Gunner

PP Sustain
Usable Weapon(s)
Open Skill Tree

Offers great PP recovery by improving PP regen from attacking enemies
(144% attack PP regen, 120% against bosses, includes grenades)
Gunner sub can be used against bosses, but Force & Fighter would perform better if you have issues managing grenades.
TMGs can be ignored as they don't offer much compared to Rifle/Launcher.
