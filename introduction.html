Weapon Overview

Assault Rifles

Mid-range mobile assaulter

Rifle is a middle-range specialist that relies on staying within optimal distance for maximum effectiveness. Allows access to Weak Bullet, which places a marker on enemies for +20% more damage.
Because of their fast animations, they are best at dealing with enemy aggro.

Optimal Distance Mechanic Explained Sample Rifle Rotation

Grenade Launchers

Slow, powerful demolitionist

Launchers are the heavy-duty damage dealers that relies on committing attacks for maximum DPS.
Relies on good PP management to sustain their main damage option, Fear Eraser
Because of their sluggish animations, they shine best during sandbag situations.

Photon Arts

Assault Rifles

Raging Shot

Main DPS Skill

Shoots a explosive round. Can be charged to pierce through enemies.
Uncharged version has better tracking on mobile enemies, but has worse PP efficiency.

Homing Dart

Main AoE Skill

Locks up to 10 targets, before firing off homing shots.

Blaze

Evasion Skill
Repositioning Tool

Twirls around targets before firing a powerful shot. Can be held to go into a movable shooting stance.
Great for kiting targets (and reinforcing Weak Bullet while soloing), but loses to other PAs in a sandbag situation.

Grenade Launchers

Fallen Impact

Situational

Fires a delayed mortar that lands after 2 seconds. Only one can exist at any time.

Fear Eraser

Main DPS Skill

Channels a powerful laser that continuously drains PP while held.
Adds a secondary attack when held for 3 seconds or longer.

Multi-Launch

Hitscan

Fires a barrage of rockets that has great tracking against enemies.
Has great PP efficiency, and can be dash-cancelled up until the 6th shot.

Gearing Up

Budget

Evolcoat Series

With a 1% difference behind Stragga while being much easier to obtain, this is a great pick up for Rangers looking to stay within budget.
The +20% faster Photon Blast shines better in mobbing-focused quests, as most boss fights tends too fast for you to capitalize on additional PB uses.

Soulspring Unit
Fixa Attack recommended

Min-max

Stragga Series

Currently the strongest series besides the Resurge; rewards proper positioning by greatly boosting your damage at the cost of making you fragile. If you do commit to a Launcher attack like Fear Eraser and you die with this weapon out, 99% of the time it's your fault.

Ferocious Form/Berserk Unit
Fixa Attack recommended

Quality of Life

Resurge Series

Able to multi-weapon both rifle and launcher, has crit rate bonus making it potentially the strongest out of the bunch in terms of raw output and versatility. Requires you to activate the potential often to be competitive, otherwise stick with other weapons.

Flickered Form/Dynamo Unit


Swap Weapon

Greisen Series

A gimmicky weapon that shines as a swap weapon; the additional attack from the potential takes 10 seconds to detonate, but its notably strong with the added bonus of procc'ing elemental downs for more DPS opportunities! It has a 20s cooldown, but the extra attack refreshes even while on a different weapon! The low base attack holds it back from being competitive though.
Solo Rangers will have issues upkeeping Weak Bullet with this weapon due to Launcher's sluggish animations.

Valiant Form/Valorous Unit

Viable

Vialto Series

PP recovery both active and natural by 10%. This affects Launcher's WA, which is good because it can multi-weapon with Talis, which also affects its PP regen options as well - making Fear Eraser more sustainable. If you invested in it already, its best not to go with the Resurge Series and just stick with the Talis combo.

Spirited Form/Meditation Unit
Fixa Attack recommended

Viable

Foursis Series

A weapon that greatly boosts survivability due to its -% damage reduction when at max HP. Paired with NGS gear it's pretty bulky, but doesn't reward players who can dodge consistently. Middle of the pack damage, but competitive nonetheless with Fixa prefixes.

Fortified Form/Bastion Unit
Fixa Attack recommended

New Game Bonus

Lightweaver Cras Series

PSO2 players with a fully kitted Lightweaver Cras Series can skip starting NGS weapons, as their weapon provides up to 8%+ power and other bonuses from abilities like Raising Pursuit, which grants +50% more PA/Tech damage for 5 seconds when hitting with any PA/Tech. Fixa prefixes on 3* weapons roughly matches Cras' power (before additional bonuses) and is generally cheap, so don't get too attached to your weapon.

Class Combos

Recommended

Force

PP Battery
Usable Weapon(s)
Open Skill Tree

Offers great PP economy by boosting your natural PP regen through passives and actives
(150% passively, multiplies by 600% when activating PP Convert)
Note that you can't regen PP while using PAs.
Also allows rod, talis, and tech access to exploit element weaknesses for additional stuns.

Gunner

PP Sustain
Usable Weapon(s)
Open Skill Tree

Offers great PP recovery by improving PP regen from attacking enemies
(144% attack PP regen, 120% against bosses, includes grenades)
Gunner sub can be used against bosses, but Force & Fighter would perform better if you have issues managing grenades.
TMGs can be ignored as they don't offer much compared to Rifle/Launcher.
