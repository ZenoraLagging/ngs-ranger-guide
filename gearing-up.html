Multi-Weapon System

Should I do it?

Ranger greatly benefits from having a Rifle + Launcher (or Launcher + Rifle, in some cases) as their multi-weapon combo as it opens up lots of option, such as having Weak Bullet access on a Launcher, seamlessly transitioning between Rifle & Launcher PAs, etc.
For other weapons, it depends on your playstyle and use cases.

Weapons

Min-max

Rugged

HEY REMEMBER NEMESIS AND RAVEN SERIES? NOW IT'S BACK!
Currently the strongest series at this time of writing, Rugged has separate effects, whichever weapon is the base will take effect (when multi-weaponing):

Rifle offers Citadel Unit, which generates up to 3 40% damage resistant barrier every 30 seconds (kills lower the cooldown by 2s),
while Launcher offers Gyrating Unit, which grants 30% PP reduction for 20 seconds when consuming 300 PP (10s cooldown).

It's personal preference on which weapon you want inherit as base, but some considerations:
1. Kvaris region hurts and Rangers are very vulnerable due to animation locks so damage resistance is always useful, but
2. PP reduction allows for longer rotations without needing to refill PP, but Fear Eraser and Blaze Shot (don't use this shitty PA regardless) is not affected by PP Reduction.

Variance: 50% ~ 100%
Rifle: Citadel Unit (survive longer)
Launcher: Gyrating Unit (deal more DPS)
Fixa Fatale recommended

Strong

Rokz

The current best weapon of the 6 star series; notably, the Rifle grants 20% PP recovery when taking damage, while Launcher grants a 20% Attack PP Recovery when hitting something.
You only care about Launcher to use as the base for multi-weapons, as Attack PP Recovery affects PP recovered from Launcher charged shots, counters, and all grenades!!!
Provides the highest base attack of the 6 star series as well as highest potency in general making it the strongest 6 star weapon.

Variance: 75% ~ 100%
Rifle: Revolutionary Unit
Launcher: Staccato Unit (recommended)
Fixa Attack recommended

All-Arounder

Sechetyl

The mobbing weapon. This weapon provides 22% potency and a further 6% more against enemies that are not bosses. This is the best weapon for mobbing and is also unironically better than the Evoleclipse series due to its higher base attack.

Variance: 75% ~ 100%
Trample Unit
Fixa Attack recommended

Budget

Evoleclipse

Free 6 star weapon obtainable from the Kvaris Expedition Ticket Shop if you were playing prior to Kvaris-launch; unobtainable otherwise. Provides 22% potency as well as a 100% natural PP recovery upon a successful Sidestep dodge, but natural PP recovery doesn't benefit Ranger in most scenarios. Along with the newer skills for Kvaris, this potential is almost worthless as dodging with Rifle's WA is higher DPS than a regular step dodge...

Variance: 75% ~ 100%
Elusive Unit
Fixa Attack recommended


Nothing to write here; Kvaris region hasn't introduced any new/good single weapons.

Units

Behlgren Armor

Min-max

The new gigantix armor. This unit provides 2.75% potency across all 3 stats and 13 PP but has a negative of -40 HP as well as -50% resistance to all status ailments similar to the its predecessor. This is the best unit to invest in if your main concern was Potency and PP. The resistance downside is pretty negligible considering Ranger's Skill Tree options. Has the highest defense value of the 6 star series as well but is overshadowed by the negative HP it provides.

Rare Drop from Gigantix Enemies (Kvaris Region)
Rare Drop from Battledia Purple: Kvaris Devastators
76 (at max), HP -40, PP +13, All ATK +2.75%, ALL RESIST -50%

Defrozzis Armor

PP-focused

The PP version of the Defroz series; provides 9 PP, 2% potency across all 3 stats and 20% freeze resistance. This unit can be considered if you want more PP over HP from the Sestato series.

Rare Drop from Exploration and Combat Sectors (Kvaris Region)
70 (at max), PP +9, ALL ATK +2%, +20%

Sestato Armor and Belta variant

All-Arounder

The Sestato series provides 35 HP, 5PP and 1.5% potency across all 3 stats and 20% freeze resistance, while the Belta variant drops freeze resist, slightly lower HP & PP in favor of more damage (30 HP, 4 PP, +2.25% potency for Ranged & Tech only).
Overall a well balanced unit.

Basic variant:
Rare Drop from Exploration and Combat Sectors (Kvaris Region)
Red Container (Rayjord Gorge)
71 (at max), HP +35, PP +5, ALL ATK +1.5%, +20%

Belta variant:
Rare Drop from Battledia Purple: Kvaris Devastators
Red Container (North Kvaris)
70 (at max), HP +30, PP +4, +2.25% &

Defrozza Armorr

Usable

The HP version of the Defroz series; provides +50 HP, 1% damage resistance and 1% potency across all 3 stats along with a bonus of 20% freeze resistance. Ranger prefers more PP than HP, so this armor isn't as recommended over other options, and Sestato provides better all-around stats.

Rare Drop from Exploration and Combat Sectors (Kvaris Region)
70 (at max), HP +50, ALL ATK +1%, DMG RESIST +1%, +20%
